Peptide vs Steroid Hormone Signalling

Peptide Hormones

  • Hydrophilic — cell surface receptors
  • Rapid actions (seconds–minutes)
  • Second messengers: cAMP, IP₃/DAG, tyrosine kinase

Steroid Hormones

  • Lipophilic — intracellular receptors
  • Slower actions (hours–days)
  • Genomic: transactivation & transrepression

The HPA Axis

Cortisol Transport

Fraction Percentage Binding Partner
CBG-bound ~80% Cortisol-binding globulin
Albumin-bound ~15% Serum albumin
Free (active) ~5% Unbound

Only free cortisol crosses membranes to bind the GR.

The GR Complex: Inactive State

  • HSP90 (×2) — maintains conformation
  • HSP70 — assists folding
  • Immunophilins (FKBP51/52)
  • p23 — stabilises HSP90–GR

Upon Ligand Binding:

  1. Cortisol binds → 2. HSPs dissociate → 3. GR dimerises → 4. Nuclear translocation → 5. GRE binding

Transactivation vs Transrepression

Transactivation

GR dimers → GRE binding → upregulate:

  • Annexin-1, IκBα, MKP-1, IL-10, SLPI
  • → Anti-inflammatory genes

Transrepression

GR monomers → protein–protein interaction → suppress:

  • NF-κB → TNFα, IL-1β, IL-6, COX-2
  • AP-1 → MMPs
  • → Pro-inflammatory mediator suppression

Part 2: Physiological Actions

Metabolic Effects

Carbohydrate

  • ↑ Hepatic gluconeogenesis, ↓ peripheral glucose uptake → raised blood glucose

Protein

  • ↑ Catabolism → muscle wasting, skin thinning

Lipid

  • Lipolysis (extremities) + lipogenesis (trunk) → centripetal fat redistribution

Immune & Inflammatory Actions

Anti-inflammatory

  • Inhibits PLA₂ (via annexin-1)
  • Suppresses COX-2, iNOS
  • Reduces cytokines (IL-1β, TNFα, IL-6)
  • Decreases vascular permeability
  • Inhibits leukocyte adhesion/emigration

Immunosuppressive

  • ↓ Lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils
  • ↑ Neutrophils (demargination)
  • Inhibits T-cell proliferation
  • Suppresses antibody production
  • Promotes lymphocyte apoptosis

Cardiovascular & Bone Effects

Cardiovascular

  • Permissive effect on catecholamines
  • Na⁺ retention, K⁺ excretion → hypertension

Bone

  • ↓ Osteoblast function (reduced formation)
  • ↑ Osteoclast activity (increased resorption)
  • ↓ Ca²⁺ absorption and reabsorption → osteoporosis

Part 3: Pathophysiology

Cushing’s Syndrome

Cortisol excess — 10–15 million worldwide

Causes

  • Iatrogenic (prolonged GC treatment)
  • Adrenal tumours
  • Pituitary adenoma (Cushing’s disease)
  • Ectopic ACTH (SCLC, bronchial carcinoid, etc.)

Fatal if untreated; treatable with adrenalectomy + GC replacement

Clinical Features

  • Obesity — 90%
  • Hypertension — 85%
  • Weakness — 85%
  • Emotional lability — 85%
  • Osteopenia — 80%
  • Glucose intolerance — 75%
  • Hirsutism — 75%
  • Hyperlipidaemia — 70%
  • Plethora — 70%
  • Striae — 50%
  • Kidney stones — 50%
  • Acne/bruising — 35%
  • Polyuria — 30%
  • Diabetes — 20%
  • Moon face, buffalo hump
  • Increased infection susceptibility

Addison’s Disease

Cortisol deficiency — difficult to diagnose clinically

Primary Causes (hyperpigmentation present)

  • Autoimmune, TB, infections, metastatic cancer, adrenal haemorrhage

Secondary Causes (no hyperpigmentation)

  • Pituitary tumours, inflammation, surgery, inappropriate GC withdrawal

Addisonian Crisis ⚠️

  • Severe hypotension + hyperkalaemia — life-threatening

Hyperpigmentation Mechanism

Only in primary disease (elevated ACTH). Secondary = low ACTH = no pigmentation.

Part 4: NCX-1015 Discovery

The Clinical Problem

GCs = most effective anti-inflammatory drugs, BUT long-term side effects:

  • Osteoporosis ← key target
  • GI damage
  • HPA suppression
  • Hypertension
  • Immunosuppression

Can we maintain efficacy while sparing bone?

NCX-1015 Design

Prednisolone (C₂₁H₂₈O₅, MW 360.4)

→ Two-step synthesis →

NCX-1015 (C₂₉H₃₃NO₉, MW 539.59)

  • Prednisolone 21-[(4’-nitroxymethyl)benzoate]
  • NO-donating group at C-21 via ester bond
  • NO released by esterase cleavage

NO Release: Confirmed In Vitro & In Vivo

In Vitro (PRP)

  • Time- and concentration-dependent nitrite + cGMP release
  • Esterase-dependent mechanism confirmed

In Vivo (Mouse Peritoneum)

  • Significant nitrite at 15, 30, 60 min (p≤0.05)
  • Prednisolone: negligible NO release

GR Activity Retained & Enhanced

  • NCX-1015 increased CD163 expression with greater potency than prednisolone
  • Equivalent IL-1β suppression
  • Greater HSP90 dissociation → enhanced GR nuclear translocation
  • Evidence of GR tyrosine nitration — novel post-translational mechanism

Acute Inflammation: ED₅₀ Comparison

Parameter Prednisolone NCX-1015 Improvement
PMN accumulation 9.5 mg/kg 2 mg/kg ~5×
KC chemokine >10 mg/kg 2 mg/kg >5×
iNOS/Nitrite 8 mg/kg <0.5 mg/kg >16×

NCX-1015 up to 16-fold more potent for iNOS suppression.

Chronic Inflammation: Arthritis

  • Reduced paw volume and clinical scores (p<0.05)
  • Dramatically reduced IL-1β and IL-6 (p<0.05)
  • Reduced collagen II antibodies
  • Effective i.p. and orally
  • Reduced pyridinoline (bone degradation marker) to healthy baseline

Bone Sparing: The Key Result

Treatment (1 nM) Osteoclast Resorption
Prednisolone ↑↑ ~175%
NCX-1015 ~100% (no increase)
Pred + NOC-18 (NO donor) Rescued
NCX-1015 + ODQ (GC inhibitor) Protection abolished
NCX-1016 (no NO group) ↑ like prednisolone

Mechanism: NO → guanylate cyclase → cGMP → osteoclast inhibition

Three Mechanisms of Action

  1. Additive anti-inflammatory effects — NO suppresses transcription factors, cytokines, leukocyte adhesion alongside GR actions
  2. Functional antagonism — NO via cGMP protects bone compartment against GC-induced osteoclast activation
  3. Direct GR modification — tyrosine nitration enhances HSP dissociation and GR signalling potency

Part 5: Drug Discovery Process

The Pipeline

Stage Duration
Target Identification 0.5–1 year
Target Validation 0.5–1 year
Lead Identification 0.5–1 year
Candidate Optimisation 2–3 years
Pre-Clinical 1–2 years
Clinical Trials 4–6 years
Total ~12–14 years

The Attrition Funnel

10,000–20,000 candidates → 1 approved drug

Costs per Stage (US$ millions)

  • Target validation/lead: $674M
  • Phase I: $273M | Phase II: $319M | Phase III: $314M

Phase II has the highest attrition (56%) — the translation gap / “valley of death”

Summary: Complete Lecture

  • Cortisol is regulated via the HPA axis with negative feedback
  • GR signalling involves HSP dissociation → nuclear translocation → transactivation/transrepression
  • Cortisol excess (Cushing’s) and deficiency (Addison’s) produce opposing clinical syndromes
  • NCX-1015 is a NO-releasing prednisolone that is more potent, orally active, and bone-sparing
  • The bone-sparing mechanism operates via NO → cGMP → osteoclast inhibition
  • Drug discovery takes 12–14 years, costs >$1.6 billion, with <0.01% success rate
  • NCX-1015 exemplifies successful academic–industry preclinical drug discovery
