NCX-1015: A Novel Nitric Oxide-Releasing Glucocorticoid

Unit 5, Part 4 — BIOL09044 Inflammation & Cancer

Dr Mark Paul-Clark

Nottingham Trent University

The Clinical Problem

Synthetic glucocorticoids are the most effective anti-inflammatory drugs available, used in RA, asthma, and cancer.

However, long-term therapy causes serious side effects:

  • Bone metabolism → Osteoporosis
  • GI tract damage (especially with NSAIDs)
  • HPA axis suppression (adrenal atrophy)
  • Hypertension
  • Immunosuppression → increased infection risk

The Question

Can we design a glucocorticoid that maintains anti-inflammatory efficacy but eliminates bone-damaging side effects?

Published: Paul-Clark MJ, Mancini L, Lim LHK, Burgaud J-L, Del Soldato P, Flower RJ & Perretti M

Study Aims

  1. Synthesise the novel compound NCX-1015 (prednisolone 21-[(4’-nitroxymethyl)benzoate])

  2. Assess NO release from NCX-1015 by esterases in platelet-rich plasma (in vitro) and the peritoneal cavity (in vivo)

  3. Test anti-inflammatory effects in a complement-mediated acute peritoneal inflammation model

  4. Assess effects on leukocyte rolling, adhesion and emigration in the mesenteric microvasculature

  5. Evaluate efficacy in a chronic inflammation model: collagen II-induced arthritis

The Key Questions

For NCX-1015 to succeed as a drug candidate, it must demonstrate:

  • ✅ Maintained anti-inflammatory activity (at least equivalent to prednisolone)
  • ✅ Better profile of effect (improved potency or broader actions)
  • ✅ No detrimental effect on bone
  • ✅ Acceptable side effect profile (HPA axis, GI, etc.)

NCX-1015 Synthesis

Two-Step Reaction

Step 1: Prednisolone (C₂₁H₂₈O₅, MW 360.4)

→ reacted with 4-(chloromethyl)benzoyl chloride + triethylamine (24h)

→ Prednisolone 21-(4’-chlorobenzoate)

Step 2: Intermediate compound

→ reacted with silver nitrite + acetonitrile + tetrahydrofuran (35h, dark)

→ NCX-1015 (C₂₉H₃₃NO₉, MW 539.59)

Note

The NO-donating nitroxymethyl group is attached via an ester bond at the C-21 position, allowing esterase-mediated NO release in biological systems.

SAR: Modified NO-Prednisolone Compounds

Ten nitro-ester derivatives of prednisolone were synthesised and tested for:

  • Aqueous solubility

In Vitro: NO Release from NCX-1015

Time-Dependent Release in Platelet-Rich Plasma

  • NCX-1015 released nitrite in a time-dependent manner over 60 minutes
  • SNP (sodium nitroprusside) served as positive control
  • Prednisolone alone showed no NO release

Concentration-Dependent Release

  • NCX-1015 produced concentration-dependent increases in both:
    • Nitrite levels (significant at 100–1000 µM, p≤0.05)
    • cGMP in platelets (significant at 100–1000 µM, p≤0.05)
  • Prednisolone produced neither response

Tip

The esterase-dependent mechanism was confirmed: NCX-1015 + esterase dramatically increased NO release, while prednisolone ± esterase had no effect.

In Vivo: NO Release in the Peritoneum

Mouse Peritoneal Cavity

  • NCX-1015 (5 and 10 mg/kg) produced significant time-dependent nitrite release at 15, 30, and 60 minutes (p≤0.05)
  • Prednisolone (10 mg/kg) showed negligible NO release
  • Dose-dependent: 10 mg/kg NCX-1015 > 5 mg/kg

Note

This confirmed that NCX-1015 releases NO through biological esterase activity both in vitro and in vivo, validating the compound design.

GR-Dependent Effects: CD163 and IL-1β

Methods

  • Human PBMCs isolated by Ficoll-Hypaque density gradient
  • Pre-incubated with prednisolone or NCX-1015 (10⁻⁵ to 10⁻¹⁰ M)

Results

CD163 Expression (FACS)

  • Both compounds increased CD163 in a concentration-dependent manner
  • NCX-1015 showed greater potency than prednisolone at 10⁻⁷ to 10⁻⁶ M

IL-1β Suppression (LPS-stimulated)

  • Both compounds reduced IL-1β production
  • NCX-1015 was at least as effective as prednisolone
  • Significant reduction from 10⁻⁸ M onwards

Important

NCX-1015 retains full glucocorticoid receptor-dependent activity while also releasing NO — a true dual-action compound.

Nuclear Localisation of GR

HSP90 Dissociation

  • NCX-1015 caused significantly greater dissociation of HSP90 from the GR compared to prednisolone (p<0.05)
  • Demonstrated by co-immunoprecipitation and western blotting

Immunofluorescence Microscopy

  • NCX-1015 promoted enhanced GR nuclear translocation at 30 minutes post-treatment
  • Prednisolone and NCX-1016 (lacking NO) showed less pronounced nuclear GR staining

Tip

This suggests the NO component of NCX-1015 actively potentiates GR signalling rather than simply adding a parallel anti-inflammatory mechanism.

Acute Inflammation: Zymosan Peritonitis

Methods

  • Prednisolone and NCX-1015 (0.5–10 mg/kg i.p.) administered 1h before zymosan (1 mg/mouse i.p.)
  • Peritoneal lavage at 4h; measured: PMN counts, KC, PGE₂, nitrite, iNOS, IL-1

Results — ED₅₀ Comparison

Parameter Prednisolone ED₅₀ NCX-1015 ED₅₀ Fold improvement
PMN accumulation 9.5 mg/kg 2 mg/kg ~5×
KC (chemokine) >10 mg/kg 2 mg/kg >5×
iNOS/Nitrite 8 mg/kg <0.5 mg/kg >16×
IL-1β — Comparable —
PGE₂ 4.5 mg/kg 5 mg/kg Similar

Important

NCX-1015 was up to 16-fold more potent than prednisolone for suppressing iNOS-derived NO production in acute inflammation.

NCX-1015 vs NCX-1016: Role of NO

NCX-1016 = prednisolone derivative lacking the NO group

At 5 mg/kg in zymosan peritonitis:

  • Prednisolone: reduced PMN to ~10 × 10⁶
  • NCX-1015: reduced PMN to ~6 × 10⁶ (p<0.05 vs pred)
  • NCX-1016: reduced PMN to ~10 × 10⁶ (same as prednisolone)
  • NCX-1016 + NOC-18 (NO donor): reduced PMN to ~7 × 10⁶ (p<0.05 vs pred)

Key Conclusion

The enhanced anti-inflammatory effect of NCX-1015 is specifically attributable to NO release, as confirmed by the comparable performance of NCX-1016 + exogenous NO donor.

Intravital Microscopy: Leukocyte–Endothelial Interactions

Methods

  • Prednisolone (5 mg/kg) or NCX-1015 (0.5 mg/kg — 10× lower dose)
  • Mesenteric microvasculature visualised; measured rolling velocity, adhesion, emigration

Results (vs vehicle control, IL-1β-stimulated)

Emigrated leukocytes Elevated Significant reduction*

*p≤0.05 vs vehicle control

Important

NCX-1015 at one-tenth the dose of prednisolone produced superior inhibition of leukocyte adhesion and emigration.

Chronic Inflammation: Air Pouch Model

Croton Oil-Induced Granulomatous Air Pouch

  • Daily treatment (day 3–14) with prednisolone (5 mg/kg) or NCX-1015 (5 mg/kg)
  • Measured: granuloma dry weight, carmine content (vascularity), vascular index

Results

  • Both compounds reduced granuloma weight at days 3 and 7
  • Both reduced carmine content (indicating reduced angiogenesis)
  • NCX-1015 showed a significantly higher vascular index at day 14 (p<0.05)
  • NCX-1015 at equimolar doses was at least as effective as prednisolone in chronic inflammation

Chronic Inflammation: Collagen II-Induced Arthritis

Methods

  • Bovine collagen II/Freund’s incomplete adjuvant injected intradermally in rats
  • Treatment: prednisolone or NCX-1015 (0.21 and 2.1 mg/kg) i.p. daily (d14–18)
  • Also tested oral dosing (p.o., d14–21)

Key Results

  • NCX-1015 (2.1 mg/kg) significantly reduced paw volume and clinical scores (p<0.05 vs control)
  • NCX-1015 dramatically reduced serum IL-1β (to near-untreated levels) and IL-6 (p<0.05)
  • Reduced collagen II antibodies (p<0.05 vs control)
  • Effective via both i.p. and oral routes
  • Prednisolone at equivalent doses showed less consistent efficacy

Serum Biochemistry in Arthritis Model

NCX-1015 (2.1 mg/kg) showed notable effects:

Globulin ↓ reduced p<0.05
Corticosterone ↓↓ suppressed p<0.05 (HPA axis effect)

The significant corticosterone suppression by NCX-1015 indicates HPA axis effects remain — an area requiring further investigation for clinical development.

The Critical Question: Bone Sparing?

Primary Rat Osteoclast Bone Resorption Assay

  • Osteoclasts isolated from neonatal rat femora and tibiae
  • Cultured on calcium phosphate apatite (OAAS™) plates
  • Resorption pit area measured after 24h

Results

Treatment (1 nM) Resorbed Area (% control)
Vehicle 100%
Prednisolone ~175% (p<0.01)
NCX-1015 ~100% (not different from control)
Prednisolone + NOC-18 ~100% (rescued)
NCX-1015 + ODQ ~175% (protection abolished)
NCX-1016 (no NO) ~150% (p<0.05)

Mechanism of Bone Protection

Proof of Mechanism

  • NCX-1016 (lacking NO) → increased resorption like prednisolone
  • Prednisolone + NOC-18 (NO donor) → rescued bone
  • NCX-1015 + ODQ (guanylate cyclase inhibitor) → protection abolished

The bone-sparing effect is specifically mediated by NO via the cGMP pathway.

Serum Pyridinoline: In Vivo Bone Protection

Pyridinoline = collagen cross-link released during bone degradation

In the collagen II arthritis model:

  • Control, prednisolone (both doses), NCX-1015 (0.21 mg/kg): All elevated pyridinoline (~5.5 nmol/L) vs untreated (~3.5 nmol/L)
  • NCX-1015 (2.1 mg/kg): Pyridinoline reduced to ~3.2 nmol/L (p<0.05 vs control)

Tip

This is the critical in vivo confirmation: while prednisolone exacerbated disease-associated bone degradation, NCX-1015 prevented it and restored pyridinoline to healthy baseline levels.

GR Potentiation: Receptor Binding

NCX-1015 Increases GR Binding Capacity

In both human PBMCs and U937 cells:

  • Prednisolone showed standard displacement of [³H]-dexamethasone
  • NCX-1015 at 10 µM showed dramatically increased GR binding (~90 fmol vs ~5 fmol for prednisolone)
  • Western blot confirmed increased GR protein following NCX-1015 treatment

This potentiation requires:

  • ✅ Protein synthesis (blocked by cycloheximide)
  • ✅ Cellular energy (blocked by sodium azide, deoxyglucose, DNP)
  • ✅ Guanylyl cyclase activity (blocked by ODQ)
  • ✅ GR itself (blocked by RU486)
  • ❌ NOT dependent on HSP90 ATPase (geldanamycin had no effect)

HSP Dissociation: In Vitro and In Vivo

Co-Immunoprecipitation Results

PBMCs In Vitro

  • HSP90/GR: NCX-1015 caused rapid dissociation at 30 min (p<0.05), maintained at 2h
  • HSP70/GR: NCX-1015 dissociation at 30 min; prednisolone showed increased HSP70 association at 2h

Mouse Macrophages In Vivo

  • Same pattern confirmed in vivo
  • NCX-1015 (13.8 µmol/kg) produced significantly greater HSP90 dissociation than prednisolone at 30 min
  • HSP70 dissociation also greater with NCX-1015 at 2h

Note

NCX-1015 promotes more rapid and complete HSP dissociation, facilitating enhanced GR nuclear translocation and signalling potency.

GR Nitration: A Novel Mechanism

Tyrosine Nitration of GR

In vitro: NCX-1015 (1–10 µM) caused concentration-dependent tyrosine nitration of the GR protein (~95 kDa band on immunoprecipitation + anti-nitrotyrosine blot)

In vivo: GR nitration detected in peritoneal macrophages from mice treated with NCX-1015 (both i.p. and s.c. routes)

Novel Post-Translational Mechanism

This suggests NO released from NCX-1015 directly modifies the GR protein through tyrosine nitration, potentially altering its conformation, HSP interactions, and transcriptional activity. This represents a unique mechanism not seen with conventional glucocorticoids.

NCX-1015: Three Mechanisms of Action

Summary: NCX-1015

  • NCX-1015 is a nitric oxide-releasing prednisolone derivative
  • It releases NO through esterase-mediated cleavage both in vitro and in vivo
  • It demonstrates enhanced anti-inflammatory potency (up to 16× prednisolone for iNOS suppression)
  • The NO component provides a bone-sparing effect mediated through the cGMP pathway
  • NCX-1015 potentiates GR signalling through enhanced HSP dissociation and GR tyrosine nitration
  • It is effective in acute (peritonitis, intravital microscopy) and chronic (air pouch, collagen arthritis, gouty arthritis) inflammation models
  • It is orally active
  • It spares the bone compartment — the critical advantage over conventional glucocorticoids

Self-Assessment

Q1: Why was NCX-1016 (the derivative without NO) an essential control in these studies? NCX-1016 allowed the researchers to determine whether the enhanced effects of NCX-1015 were due to the structural modification (ester linkage) or specifically to the NO-releasing capability. Since NCX-1016 behaved like prednisolone in both inflammation and bone assays, the enhanced effects of NCX-1015 could be confidently attributed to NO release.
Q2: Explain how the ODQ experiment proves the mechanism of NCX-1015’s bone-sparing effect. ODQ is a guanylate cyclase inhibitor that blocks the NO → cGMP signalling pathway. When ODQ was co-administered with NCX-1015, the bone-sparing effect was abolished — osteoclast resorption increased to prednisolone-like levels. This demonstrates that the protective effect requires NO-mediated cGMP production, not simply the structural modification of prednisolone.
Q3: Based on the serum biochemistry data, what potential concern remains for NCX-1015 clinical development? NCX-1015 caused significant corticosterone suppression, indicating HPA axis suppression comparable to or exceeding that of prednisolone. Additionally, elevated creatinine and cholesterol were observed. While the bone-sparing and enhanced anti-inflammatory effects are advantageous, HPA axis suppression and potential metabolic effects would need to be carefully evaluated in clinical trials.
